Our Philosophy

We believe the objects we live with should be honest, natural, and made to last.

The word aela comes from an old Germanic root meaning "to nourish." We believe nourishment isn't a luxury reserved for special occasions — it's a daily practice. Every meal at the table. Every morning cup of tea. Every moment of rest.

AELADAILY draws from Wabi-Sabi — the Japanese aesthetic philosophy that finds beauty in imperfection, simplicity, and the natural aging of materials. A cotton linen tablecloth that softens with each wash. A hand-woven rope basket that tells the story of its maker. A soy wax candle whose scent shifts subtly with the seasons.

We don't chase trends. We make objects that earn their place in your home through daily use — objects that become more beautiful, not less, as they live with you.

How It Began

Our Journey

2024 · The Idea

A Seed Is Planted

Living in Germany, surrounded by European craft traditions but frustrated by the flood of disposable, plastic-heavy home goods, we began imagining a different kind of store — one that sold only natural materials, made by hand, designed to be used every day.

2025 · Sourcing

Finding Our Artisans

We traveled through China's craft regions — the cotton linen mills of Shandong, the rope workshops of Yiwu, the soy candle makers of Hangzhou. We selected partners who shared our commitment to natural materials and fair labor practices.

Early 2026 · Building

Brand & Compliance

We registered our trademark, completed EU compliance (CE marking, REACH, VerpackG/LUCID registration), set up VAT structures, and built our Shopify DTC store. Every detail — from packaging design to legal pages — was crafted to reflect our wabi-sabi ethos.

2026 · Launch

AELADAILY Goes Live

Our first 50 SKUs are here. Cotton linen textiles, hand-woven decor, natural kitchen tools, and aromatherapy. We ship across Europe with plastic-free packaging and a 30-day return policy. This is just the beginning.

The Craft

Every piece carries the mark of its maker.

We work directly with small workshops and family-run studios. No middlemen, no agents, no anonymous factories. When you buy a macrame wall hanging from AELADAILY, you're supporting the hands that knotted every strand.

Our cotton linen is woven on traditional looms in Shandong, where weavers have practiced their craft for generations. Our rope baskets are hand-coiled and stitched in a small workshop with fewer than 20 artisans. Our soy candles are poured in small batches, each one labeled with its pour date.

This is slow commerce. It takes longer. It costs a little more. But the result is objects with soul — objects you'll keep for years, not seasons.

Our Commitment

Sustainability Is Not a Marketing Term

It's the foundation of everything we do. Here's what that actually means in practice.

Material Sourcing

Every material is traceable to its origin. Cotton linen from certified mills. Beeswax from European apiaries. Soy wax from non-GMO soybeans. Wood from FSC-certified forests.

Plastic-Free Packaging

Recycled kraft paper. Natural cotton twine. Compostable mailers made from corn starch. Water-activated paper tape. Zero plastic in any package, ever.

Carbon-Conscious Shipping

We consolidate shipments to reduce air freight. Sea freight for bulk inventory. Carbon offset for every package shipped. We track and publish our emissions quarterly.

Fair Labor

We pay above-market wages to our artisan partners. No child labor, no forced overtime. We visit every workshop at least once per year to verify working conditions.

EPR Compliance

Registered with Germany's LUCID/VerpackG system. EU EPR compliant for packaging, electronics, and textiles. We take responsibility for the full lifecycle of our products.

Give-Back Program

1% of annual revenue goes to environmental nonprofits. Customers can round up their order to support reforestation projects in Europe.

A Word From Us

Why we started AELADAILY

"I moved to Germany for my studies and fell in love with the European appreciation for craft — the way a wooden cutting board is passed down through generations, the way a linen tablecloth gets softer and more beautiful with each wash."

"But I also saw how much plastic and how many disposable products had crept into daily life. I wanted to offer an alternative: objects made from natural materials, by hand, that you could use every day without guilt. Objects that would age with you, not against you."

"AELADAILY is my answer to the question: what if everything in your home was natural, beautiful, and made to last?"
